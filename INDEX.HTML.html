Care Guide

Aquarium Setup & Fish Care Tips

New to fishkeeping? Follow these essentials to keep your fish happy and healthy.

🌡️

Water Temperature

Most tropical fish thrive between 24–28°C. Use a reliable aquarium heater and thermometer. Sudden changes stress fish — always acclimatize slowly.

💧

Water Quality & Filtration

Invest in a good filter. Change 20–30% of water weekly. Test for ammonia, nitrite, nitrate, and pH regularly. Clean water = healthy fish.

🍽️

Feeding Right

Feed small amounts 2x daily — only what they consume in 2–3 minutes. Overfeeding pollutes water and causes disease. Vary the diet.

🪴

Plants & Décor

Live or artificial plants provide hiding spots and reduce stress. Avoid sharp decorations. Java moss and hornwort are perfect beginner plants.

🔬

New Fish Introduction

Float the bag in your tank for 15 minutes. Add small amounts of tank water to the bag every 5 minutes for 20 minutes, then gently release the fish.

🏥

Disease Prevention

Quarantine new fish for 2 weeks before adding to community tanks. Watch for white spots, clamped fins, or erratic behaviour.

💡

Lighting Schedule

Provide 8–10 hours of light daily. Use a timer for consistency. Too much light promotes algae growth; too little stresses the fish.

📐

Tank Size Matters

Overcrowding is the #1 beginner mistake. A good rule: 1 inch of fish per gallon of water. Koi and Goldfish need larger tanks — minimum 40L per fish.
